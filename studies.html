AFiRM Study

Active · Sept 2020 – Mar 2027

Application of Functional Renal MRI in CKD (AFiRM)

NIHR EME Grant · £1,975,786 · ClinicalTrials.gov: NCT04238299

The AFiRM study aims to determine whether multiparametric renal MRI can provide structural and functional assessment of the kidneys to give prognostic information and guide treatment options. The two primary research questions are: Can multiparametric renal MRI differentiate CKD progression from stable disease? And can MRI determine the type and severity of the dominant mechanisms of CKD?

Clinical Questions

CKD Progression & Disease Mechanisms

Can MRI identify who will progress to end-stage kidney disease (ESKD)? Can MRI characterise the type and severity of underlying CKD mechanisms to guide treatment?

MRC Partnership Grant · Completed

UKRIN-MAPS

Completed · Sept 2018 – Sept 2022

MRI Acquisition and Processing Standardisation (UKRIN-MAPS)

MRC Partnership Grant · £795,786

UKRIN-MAPS developed harmonised approaches to multiparametric renal MRI across GE, Philips, and Siemens 3T scanners. The project implemented a "travelling kidney" reproducibility study, established an open-source data analysis centre (XNAT-based), and built a normative data set of 50 healthy volunteers. It directly enabled the AFiRM study and produced the open-source UKAT software toolkit.

Biomarkers Measured

Multiparametric Protocol

T1 mapping, T2 mapping, diffusion imaging (DWI/IVIM), ASL perfusion (multi-TI, FAIR, pCASL), BOLD R2* (oxygenation), phase-contrast haemodynamics, morphology (TKV, cortex/medulla volumes), and magnetisation transfer.

Data Infrastructure

XNAT Data Sharing Platform

A customised XNAT-based platform with meta-data fields for renal MRI data storage and sharing, including SOP and training protocols plus phantom QC procedures.

Open Source

Scientific Background

Why Functional Renal Imaging?

AKI

Acute Kidney Injury

AKI affects 10% of hospital admissions — around 500,000 people per year in England — with mortality above 20%. Functional MRI could identify who is at risk of progressing to chronic kidney disease after an acute episode.

CKD

Chronic Kidney Disease

1.8 million people in England live with CKD. It can progress to kidney failure requiring dialysis or transplantation, and is associated with markedly elevated cardiovascular risk. MRI offers a window into disease mechanisms beyond eGFR alone.

Transplant

Renal Transplantation

At least one episode of acute allograft dysfunction occurs in approximately 30–40% of recipients. Early, non-invasive characterisation of dysfunction is crucial — functional MRI could provide this without biopsy.
