Our Mission

Transforming how the kidney is seen — and understood

UKRIN brings together major UK renal MRI research centres through a national group of MR physicists, radiologists, and clinicians dedicated to developing imaging methods to study the kidney. We co-ordinate activities across human kidney MRI in chronic kidney disease, AKI, transplant, dialysis, and cancer — while expanding into preclinical MRI, CT, ultrasound, and whole-body imaging in kidney disease.

◉

Harmonise Methods

Build a shared framework for kidney imaging acquisition, harmonisation, and AI-based reconstruction across all UK centres and vendors.

◈

Multicentre Research

Generate and deliver research proposals for clinical studies involving imaging, providing expertise to the wider UK Kidney Research Consortium (UKKRC).

◇

Clinical Translation

Generate evidence and grow clinical endorsement to support adoption of new imaging approaches from bench to bedside and into routine practice.

◆

International Links

Connect with RENALMRI.org and the ISMRM Renal MRI Study Group to align UK research within the global renal imaging community.
