Meeting Report

15th UKRIN Meeting — Nottingham

The 15th national meeting of UKRIN took place in Nottingham in June 2026, bringing together MR physicists, radiologists, and clinicians from across the network. Sessions covered AFiRM and iBEAT study updates, standardisation of MRI biomarkers, clinical topics including transplant and multiorgan imaging, new AI techniques, and BRC2028 funding opportunities.

June 2026 · Nottingham

Funding Opportunity

Horizon 2027 Health Call — Deadline Approaching

HORIZON-HLTH-2027-02-DISEASE-01-two-stage focuses on innovative healthcare interventions for non-communicable diseases. UKRIN members are encouraged to consider collaborative applications. Discussed at the 15th meeting afternoon session.

Study Milestone

AFiRM Reaches 308 Two-Year Scans

The AFiRM study has now completed two-year follow-up scanning for 308 of the 420+ enrolled CKD patients, a major milestone for the NIHR EME-funded multicentre trial running until March 2027.

2026 · NIHR EME · NCT04238299

Network Growth

Expanding Beyond Kidney MRI

UKRIN is formally expanding its scope to include preclinical MRI, CT and ultrasound modalities, and whole-body imaging in kidney disease — covering cardiac, brain, muscle, and abdominal imaging in the context of renal conditions.

2026 · Network Update

BRC Collaboration

NIHR BRC Network Imaging Opportunities

UKRIN discussed BRC2028 collaborative and network grant opportunities at the 15th meeting, with focus on transdiagnostic imaging, PPI and inclusivity activities, training, and standardisation across NIHR Biomedical Research Centres.

2026 · NIHR BRC

Perspectum Partnership for Biomarker Standardisation

Collaboration with Perspectum was discussed at the 15th meeting to explore how their imaging analysis tools could support UKRIN's MRI biomarker standardisation work across member centres.
